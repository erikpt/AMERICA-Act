# UHAA Multi‑Year Projection (2026–2033) — Phased Cost Control + MOE Phase‑In

**Outlay growth cap (phased, per Section 5(d)(1)):**
- Plan Years 1–2 (2026–2027): Core CPI + 2.5 pp (~4.7%/yr) — transition and infrastructure build
- Plan Years 3–5 (2028–2030): Core CPI + 1.5 pp (~3.7%/yr) — initial implementation
- Plan Years 6+ (2031+): Core CPI + 1.0 pp (~3.2%/yr) — maturing system
- Long-run target (Year 11+): Core CPI + 0.5 pp (~2.9%/yr)

**Employer MOE phase-in (per Section 7(a)(2)–(3)):**
- Plan Year 2 (2026): 5.0% of payroll → $665B
- Plan Year 3 (2027): 6.25% of payroll → $869B
- Plan Year 4+ (2028+): 7.5% of payroll → full rate

**Other inflow growth:** Payroll base 4.5%/yr. Medicare B/D and Medicaid MOE 2.9%/yr. Co-pays 2.0%/yr.

**Year 1 (2026) base reflects:** (a) partial reform savings realization (~$130B of eventual $430B), and (b) Employer MOE at 5%, producing lean but positive first-year operations. HTF opening reserves are supplemented by the Medicare HI Trust Fund balance transfer (~$225B) per Section 7(f). Figures in **billions**.

**HTF reserve adequacy:**
- End of Year 1: $62B operating surplus + ~$225B HI Trust Fund transfer = ~$287B reserves (~0.6 months of outlays)
- End of Year 2: ~$261B cumulative surplus + HI reserves = well above 1-month floor
- End of Year 3: ~$666B cumulative surplus; minimum reserve floor (Section 5(g)(5)) comfortably met

> **vs. prior models:**
> - Original (flat CPI+0.5pp, Day 1 savings, full MOE from Year 1): 8-yr surplus ~$8.2T, Year 1 balance +$694B
> - After phased growth cap only: 8-yr surplus ~$4.0T, Year 1 balance +$394B
> - **This model (phased cap + MOE phase-in):** 8-yr surplus ~$3.5T, Year 1 balance +$62B
>
> The progression reflects honest transition economics. Year 1 is lean by design — matching how Medicare (1966), Part D (2006), and comparable international single-payer launches operated in their first years.
